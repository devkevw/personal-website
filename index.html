Hi, I'm Kevin.

I'm a student at McGill University, from Montreal, Canada.

I was born in Toronto, but my family relocated to Montreal for work when I was very young. It is now the place I proudly call home.

Over the last three years, I’ve been working hard towards my Bachelor of Science degree, majoring in statistics and computer science, and minoring in finance. With one year left to go, I’m uncertain about my plans after graduation. However, I am excited for the possibilities that lie ahead!

Hobbies.

Some things I like to do in my free time.

Sports

I grew up playing sports. Throughout my childhood, I played basketball, soccer, ran track, swam, skied and skated. In high school, I started going to the gym and unfortunately herniated a disc in my lower back. Safe to say, I will never be the same athletically as I once was. Nowadays, I find myself watching the NBA and the NFL, and run an intramural basketball team called the Bob the Builders at my university. Ball if life.

Board Games

My friends an I occasionally like to kick it back with some board games. Our favorite at the moment is Catan. Some would call me the Catan King. Other "board" games (though whether or not they are considered board games is up for debate) are chess and poker (I don't consider poker a board game, but it felt fitting to put it here).

Reading

I used to read a lot as a child. I remember going to the library every weekend to borrow new books. However, when high school hit, I detested reading, mainly because our school had summer reading to do over the break. However, these days, I find myself reading before bed whenever I have the time. Some of my favorite books include The Psychology of Money by Morgan Housel, How to Win Friends and Influence People by Dale Carnegie, and 1984 and Animal Farm by George Orwell.

Writing
